SHIFTING MEANINGS

2018 – 19
Exhibited at Dutch Design Week 2019
Exhibited at De Waag 2019

Textiles are often used to define certain spaces, shifting from two dimensional sheets to three-dimensional structures.
This way they become a tool and a medium to reinterpret, modify and understand our surroundings.
In a similar way, flags are just rectangular pieces of fabric that define certain spaces (political, geographical, physical, economical, virtual, commercial, ideal, ...) and hold several meanings. Their structure of values is related to their materiality, in which every color, shape, sewing stitch or mark acts as a symbol for something else.
A flag can represent entire nations, patriotism, identity, loyalty, sense of belonging, and hence has to be treated in a respectful and honorable manner.

How we should treat a flag is described in what is called a “flag-code”, and it includes instructions on how to treat a flag the best way possible.
